## Design Context

### Users
Affluent Arabic-speaking women in Jordan and the wider Gulf region, browsing on mobile to research and book laser/skincare treatments. Secondary audience: English-speaking expats and international clients. Used during research and consideration phases — emotional state is aspirational (wanting to feel beautiful, confident) not urgent. Booking is a high-trust action.

### Brand Personality
Luminous, precise, aspirational. The brand should feel like a premium clinic that knows its craft — not a spa, not a hospital, not a generic beauty brand.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first luxury aesthetic with the "Laser Glow" palette: pink (#FF0057), orange (#FF7A00), purple (#6A00FF). Apple-inspired restraint in layout and typography. Inter (EN) + Cairo (AR). The existing glassmorphism nav is intentional. Gradient text on `.laser-text` is a deliberate brand identifier — treat as intentional, flag lightly only.

Anti-references: generic Squarespace clinic templates, cold/clinical medical whites, generic card grids with stock photos.

### Design Principles
1. **Luminous restraint** — Motion and color should feel like light, not noise
2. **Bilingual equality** — Arabic RTL and English LTR must feel equally designed, not translated
3. **Trust through detail** — Luxury is communicated through micro-details, not decoration
4. **Mobile-first intimacy** — Most users are on phone; the experience should feel personal
5. **Accessibility as respect** — WCAG AA minimum; motion respects prefers-reduced-motion
